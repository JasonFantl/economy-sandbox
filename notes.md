
I would like the player to feel that they can make changes to the economy (as a limited player, although we should also have a god-mode). Then they can step back and watch the economy over time. In an ideal world we could track the decisions of every agent and see what caused which price change. 

We would also like to give the impression that this system could be dropped in to a video game like Skyrim, allowing a living economy.

The types fo actions I want to see the player make to influence the economy:
* Flood market with a good from player
* burn down forest
* block a trade route
* Place termites in wood store
* Post newspaper that changes opinion, or start a rumor (maybe have opinion dynamics?)
* advance technology for more efficient wood production
* Influence king to change a policy (tax, subsidizing work, large gov projects)
* many more



We also need locations that the player can interact with:
Forest -> where wod comes from
Mine -> where metal comes from
Farm -> Farmers make food

Actions:
(Each action can be performed with no tool inefficiently, or with a tool more efficiently)
* farm food
* cut down wood
* mine metal
* turn wood and metal into tool
* turn wood into chair
* turn wood into bed
* turn metal into weapon
